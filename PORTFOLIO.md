## Interview Talking Points

Use these when presenting the project in interviews:

### "Walk me through this project"

> "I built a simulated enterprise fraud intelligence platform to demonstrate both
> technical Power BI skills and domain knowledge in fraud analytics. I designed it
> to reflect a real-world scenario — a fictional fintech company processing $14B
> in monthly payments — with the kind of KPIs, dashboards, and operational processes
> you'd actually see in a fraud operations center.
>
> The technical centerpiece is a star schema data model feeding 6 dashboard pages
> and over 60 DAX measures. But what I'm most proud of is the domain depth — I
> documented the detection methodology, the SOC alert playbooks, the anomaly
> detection framework, and an executive risk brief that I'd actually feel comfortable
> presenting to a board."

### "What fraud domain knowledge did this require?"

> "I had to understand the full fraud operations stack — from how different channels
> carry different risk profiles (wire fraud at 9% vs. card POS at 0.3%), to the
> regulatory obligations (SAR filing thresholds, OFAC screening, NACHA return codes),
> to the detection methods used in real production environments (rule engines, ML
> models, network graph analysis). I also researched real fraud typologies like BEC,
> synthetic identity bust-outs, and SIM swap ATOs to make the scenarios realistic."

### "How does the ML monitoring dashboard work?"

> "The ML Model Performance page tracks Precision, Recall, and F1 score over time
> using DAX measures I wrote to compute a confusion matrix from labeled transaction
> data. There's also a What-If parameter that lets you drag a threshold slider and
> immediately see how Precision, Recall, F1, and estimated false positive cost change
> — which is exactly the kind of tool a model validation team would use before
> changing the alert threshold in production."

### "Why the dark theme?"

> "Fraud operations centers typically use dark-themed monitors because analysts
> are staring at screens for 8–12 hours. High contrast on dark backgrounds reduces
> eye strain and makes status indicators (red/amber/green) pop more clearly. I
> designed the full color system with semantic meaning: the P0–P4 severity palette
> is consistent across every page, so an analyst can read status at a glance without
> thinking about what color means what."
